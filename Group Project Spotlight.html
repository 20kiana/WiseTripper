Staycation Spotlight

Check out some of the UK's top staycation recommendations and get inspired for a local trip!

Why you should visit Lynton

Lynton is a beautiful, quiet, scenic town tucked amongst the coast of Devon. There are many hikes to choose from, some amongst the coast and some through the woodlands. On a clear sunny day you can see Wales from this part of the coast and take Instagram worthy pictures. The Charlie Friday’s Coffee shop sells the best afternoon tea perfect for anytime of day. The large scones come with fist fulls of clotted cream and jam. Lynton is also famous for having the highest and steepest water powered railway in the world (definitely worth the experience if you’re not scared of heights).

Brighton is the best

The thing I love about Brighton (& there is many) is the people. They are much more friendly than your typical Londoner, tend to walk to the beat of their own drum, if you know what I mean. The music and arts scene is amazing ( + there is over 326 Pubs), The atmosphere is always vibrant & people tend to show a kindness and openess towards one another. You can get lost strolling through the North Lanes & spend an entire day weaving in and out of amazing little stores, enjoy a day at the beach and pier, go and see one of the many live performances/shows. the list goes on and on. For anyone considering a visit to Brighton or if you were like me & had never heard of the place, I strongly reccomend that you make the visit.

Lake District is ideal for rejuvenation

a perfect place for people who love to drive on hills. a place full of lakes and beautiful sceneries. a place for relaxation, mountain trekking, cycling, canoeing. if these activities are not your cup of tea, so just sit idle in your hotel room and relax.
